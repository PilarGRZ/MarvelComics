History

Marvel Beginnings :

Marvel Comics, founded in 1939 as Timely Publications, began as a comic book publisher featuring characters such as Captain America during World War II.

The Golden Age:

In the 1960s, Marvel entered its "Golden Age" with the creation of iconic characters as Spider-Man, Iron Man, Thor, Hulk and the X-Men, under the direction of Stan Lee and Jack Kirby.

The Comics Revolution:

Marvel revolutionized comics by giving depth to its characters with everyday problems, humanize superheroes and create a shared universe in which they coexist.

Success in the media:

Starting in the 2000s, Marvel enjoyed unprecedented success in film and television with the launch of the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), which includes titles such as "The Avengers" and "Black Panther."

Lasting Legacy:

Over the decades, Marvel has influenced popular culture and created a fan base. passionate, becoming one of the most recognized and successful comics publishers in the world. world.
